Academic Programs

Comprehensive degree programs in cybersecurity and computer science

Choose Your Path

Our academic programs are designed to provide comprehensive education in cybersecurity, from foundational knowledge to advanced research. Each program combines theoretical understanding with practical application to prepare graduates for successful careers in cybersecurity.

Degree Programs

Undergraduate

B.Sc. Computer Science

Cybersecurity Specialization

A comprehensive 4-year undergraduate program that provides a strong foundation in computer science with specialized focus on cybersecurity principles and practices.

Program Highlights:
  • 4-year degree program (8 semesters)
  • 120 credit hours total
  • Industry internship required
  • Capstone project in final year
  • Professional certification preparation
Duration: 4 years Credits: 120
Masters

M.Sc. Cybersecurity

Advanced Cybersecurity Studies

An intensive 2-year graduate program designed for professionals seeking advanced knowledge in cybersecurity, including research and specialized applications.

Program Highlights:
  • 2-year degree program (4 semesters)
  • 36 credit hours total
  • Thesis or project option
  • Advanced research opportunities
  • Industry collaboration projects
Duration: 2 years Credits: 36
Doctoral

Ph.D. Cybersecurity

Research & Innovation

A research-intensive doctoral program for scholars pursuing advanced research in cybersecurity, preparing future leaders and researchers in the field.

Program Highlights:
  • 3-4 year research program
  • 60 credit hours minimum
  • Original research dissertation
  • Teaching assistantship opportunities
  • International collaboration
Duration: 3-4 years Credits: 60+

Admission Requirements

B.Sc. Computer Science

Undergraduate Requirements
  • WAEC/NECO with 5 credits including Mathematics and English
  • JAMB UTME score of 200 and above
  • Post-UTME screening examination
  • Physics and Chemistry at O'Level
  • Age requirement: 16 years minimum
Application Deadline: March 31, 2025

M.Sc. Cybersecurity

Masters Requirements
  • Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field
  • Minimum CGPA of 3.0 (Second Class Upper)
  • GRE scores (recommended)
  • Two academic references
  • Statement of purpose
Application Deadline: April 30, 2025

Ph.D. Cybersecurity

Doctoral Requirements
  • Master's degree in relevant field
  • Minimum CGPA of 3.5
  • Research proposal
  • Three academic references
  • Interview with admission committee
Application Deadline: May 31, 2025

Ph.D. Cybersecurity Program Requirements
Program Structure
Coursework Phase (Years 1-2)
  • Core Courses (24 credits): Advanced topics in cybersecurity research
  • Elective Courses (12 credits): Specialized areas of interest
  • Research Seminars (6 credits): Current topics and methodologies
  • Comprehensive Examination: Written and oral exams
Research Phase (Years 2-4)
  • Dissertation Proposal: Original research proposal defense
  • Original Research: Independent research under faculty supervision
  • Publications: Minimum 3 peer-reviewed publications
  • Dissertation Defense: Final oral defense of research
Research Areas
  • AI and Machine Learning Security
  • Blockchain and Cryptocurrency Security
  • IoT and Mobile Security
  • Digital Forensics and Incident Response
  • Cryptography and Privacy
  • Network Security and Infrastructure
